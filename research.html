Getting started

Write 5–10 keywords plus synonyms. Track searches and promising citations in a simple note or spreadsheet.

Reach the library via your portal

Begin in a broad index (Academic Search, Web of Science), then move to field-specific databases. Filter by year, peer-reviewed, and subject.

Plan realistic timelines

Be realistic

Reading, note-taking, and synthesis take time. Block calendar time and allow for interlibrary-loan delays.

Estimate time to obtain, skim, read closely, and integrate each source. Prioritize the most relevant items first.

Requesting resources

If full text isn’t available, use the library’s request tools (e.g., interlibrary loan) and save the citation so you can follow up.

Local libraries

When deadlines are tight, visiting a nearby academic or public library may be faster than waiting for shipping.

Web search techniques

Use phrase quotes, AND/OR, and site filters (site:.gov). Follow citations forward and backward to expand your set.
